about me

I grew up in New Hampshire and moved to Philadelphia six years ago. Currently, I live in West Philly with three roommates and a cat who has his own Facebook page.

I've been interested in writing and visual arts as long as I can remember— I've always been passionate about the act of bringing life and expression into an idea, and the puzzle of working out how it should be put together so the audience will experience the same story and emotion that I have in my head. To me, web development is very much the same.

Writing a screenplay involves translating a concept into a more concrete story outline and then into the format of a script. A website has to go from a brief to mock-ups and then into code, and personally I think they present a lot of similar challenges— a screenwriter has to remember the constraints of film (you can't write that the audience smells something, for example) and a programmer has to consider the strengths and weaknesses of the coding languages they're using. Story-tellers have to plan what information they give out, and in what order, and how, very strategically. I think good UI/UX design should do the same.
